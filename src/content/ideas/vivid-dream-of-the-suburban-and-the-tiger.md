Tony experienced this vivid dream on 2026-07-12 and shared it on 2026-07-13 as a personal reflective note for the garden.

He had fallen asleep and found himself preparing to go somewhere, though the destination was unclear. He had called an Uber, or thought he had, and someone came to pick him up in a huge vehicle, like a Chevy Suburban. He got in, and they headed toward wherever they were supposed to be going.

At some point there was a stop. The stop seemed connected to some kind of escort situation, though the details were not clear. Two fly Black girls appeared in the scene and were friendly; they did not do anything threatening. Then some guys appeared too, saying they were from G-Unit. Their message was not hostile. They warned Tony not to just get into a random car and reminded him to stay aware of his surroundings, because someone could have taken him anywhere. They said they were going to the airport, which seemed to fit the big-car context.

During that stretch, the danger shifted. Tony almost got killed by a truck, not because of the guys, but because the driver seemed foolish. After that, somehow, the dream moved to Zimbabwe, back at his parents' house. He had all his things from Canada with him. They started playing FIFA through his PC, and he had to look for another controller. The whole time, he felt woozy.

Then the dream became much stranger. Tony saw a tiger inside the house and ran away from it. He noticed damage in the walls and thought the tiger had caused it, though the cause was uncertain. He ran away, woke up, and was left with the feeling that the whole thing was strange but vivid.

## Possible themes, not conclusions

These are only tentative readings of the dream, not facts, predictions, diagnoses, or supernatural claims.

- Entering the huge unknown vehicle may point toward a feeling of surrendering control before the route or driver is fully known.
- The G-Unit warning may tentatively reflect a situational-awareness lesson: friendly or familiar energy does not remove the need to know where you are going and who has control.
- The near miss with the truck may carry the feeling of danger arriving from the side, through someone else's foolishness rather than through obvious enemies.
- Returning to Zimbabwe with possessions from Canada may suggest two life contexts overlapping: parents' home, old familiarity, and current adult possessions in the same room.
- FIFA through the PC feels like a nostalgic anchor inside the disorientation, a familiar ritual in the middle of a shifting dream landscape.
- The wooziness matters because it colors the whole sequence; Tony is moving through familiar and unfamiliar spaces without feeling fully steady.
- The tiger inside the home may represent danger appearing inside a place that should feel safe, but the note should keep that as a question rather than a conclusion.
- The damaged walls and uncertain causality may reflect how the dream linked fear to evidence quickly, while still leaving open whether the tiger actually caused the damage.
